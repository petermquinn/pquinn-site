About Me
Not your typical
finance person.

I grew up in Newton, MA and spent my summers in Brewster on the Cape — where I picked up my first jobs, learned to work hard early, and developed a healthy respect for 4-year-olds (more on that later). I made my way to Madison, Wisconsin for four years of business school and genuinely cold winters, then came back to Boston and built a career in FP&A.

My work is the kind that rarely gets a headline but tends to matter a lot: the model that changes how leadership thinks about a product line, the board deck that actually gets read, the methodology that makes two companies' data finally speak the same language.

Outside of spreadsheets, I'm usually skiing, running, or thinking about the intersection of psychology and decision-making — which, it turns out, is pretty useful in finance too.
